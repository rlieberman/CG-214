Since I was 13 and playing in really bad punk bands, I have been experimenting with HTML. Design, and making things look good has never come easy to me, so even though I understood mark-up language, I tried to stay away.

But I went to grad school to make stories interactive, so I was going to learn to code! And I gravitated towards web-based projects, so I had to get better at HTML and CSS to properly learn JavaScript.

And now, here I am teaching people how to use it! And I hope to do a good job and make people like it. Since my learning has largely been focused on tackling what I need to know how to do in order to make an interactive project. It's been a learning experience for me to teach it from the ground up.

Learning a code editor is an important part of all of this, and using Brackets has been very fun. I installed an extension called Emmet, and I'm starting to memorize shortcuts. For example, if I want to add a comment, instead of typing everything out, on a mac I can just do command+forward slash.
